Who We Are

The African Drylands Nexus Research Center (ADNRC) is a Kenya-based research NGO dedicated to interdisciplinary research in Africa's arid and semi-arid regions.

We generate applied knowledge, data, and decision-support tools that address persistent challenges of water scarcity, climate variability, ecosystem degradation, and livelihood vulnerability across dryland socio-ecological systems.

Our work bridges science, policy, and practice to support sustainable development and climate resilience in some of the world's most vulnerable ecosystems.

Our Mission & Vision

Core Research Themes

Our interdisciplinary research focuses on the interconnected challenges facing Africa's drylands through the Water-Energy-Food-Ecosystems (WEFE) nexus approach.

Water Security

Sustainable assessment and management of surface and groundwater resources in dryland and arid environments.

Food Systems & Livelihoods

Enhancing agricultural productivity, food security, and sustainable livelihoods in vulnerable dryland communities.

Sustainable Energy Systems

Research on renewable energy deployment, system performance, and integration for policy and planning.

Ecosystems & Biodiversity

Study of dryland ecosystem dynamics, degradation processes, and restoration pathways.

Climate Variability & Change

Climate risk analysis, drought assessment, and adaptation planning in dryland regions.

Nexus Integration

Integrated modeling and analysis of WEFE nexus interactions for sustainable development planning.

Focus Regions

Our work spans across Africa's drylands, with focused research in key regions facing unique challenges and opportunities.

Northern Kenya

Applied research and pilot initiatives on the Water-Food-Energy-Ecosystems nexus in arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Horn of Africa

Transboundary water management, drought resilience, and climate adaptation in cross-border dryland systems.

Sahel Region

Comparative research on dryland systems, shared natural resources, and climate resilience across the Sahel.
