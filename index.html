Who We Are

The African Drylands Nexus Research Center (ADNRC) is a Kenya-based research NGO focused on interdisciplinary research in Africa's arid and semi-arid regions.

Our Approach

Core Research Themes

Water Security

Sustainable assessment and management of surface and groundwater resources in dryland and arid environments, including hydrological modeling and water governance.

Food Systems & Livelihoods

Research on climate-resilient agriculture, pastoral systems, food security, and livelihood diversification strategies for dryland communities.

Sustainable Energy Systems

Research on renewable energy deployment, system performance, and integration for policy and planning in off-grid and remote dryland areas.

Ecosystems & Biodiversity

Study of dryland ecosystem dynamics, degradation processes, restoration pathways, and biodiversity conservation in changing climates.

Climate Variability & Change

Climate risk analysis, drought assessment, adaptation planning, and resilience building in dryland regions facing increasing climate pressures.

Horn of Africa

Transboundary research on shared natural resources, climate resilience, and conflict-sensitive approaches to water, energy, and food security in the Horn of Africa region.

African Drylands Network

Comparative research across African dryland systems, knowledge exchange, and capacity building through our pan-African network of research institutions and partners.

Data, Models, and Tools

Environmental Data

Open-access climate, hydrology, land use, and socioeconomic datasets curated for dryland research and decision-making across multiple spatial and temporal scales.

Integrated Models

Process-based and system-level nexus models for scenario analysis, impact assessment, and policy evaluation in dryland socio-ecological systems.

Decision Support

Interactive tools and dashboards for planning, policy analysis, and community engagement to support sustainable dryland development.
