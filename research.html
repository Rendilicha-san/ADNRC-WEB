Our Research

ADNRC focuses on knowledge and tools that help dryland regions adapt to climate change, improve water and food security, and restore fragile ecosystems. Our work combines field studies, advanced models, and community engagement to deliver actionable solutions.

Themes Projects Partnerships

Research Themes

Water Security:
Groundwater assessment, sustainable use, and drought resilience planning.
Food Systems:
Innovations in dryland agriculture, pastoralist livelihoods, and nutrition security.
Climate & Energy:
Climate risk modeling, renewable energy solutions, and carbon management.
Ecosystem Restoration:
Land rehabilitation, biodiversity protection, and nature-based solutions.
Nexus Research:
Integrated water–energy–food–ecosystem analysis for policy and planning.

Flagship Projects

SWAT+ for Water Security Planning:
Enhancing drought resilience for pastoralist livelihoods.
Groundwater Management:
Using isotopes and models to ensure sustainable use of aquifers.
Climate–Water–Ecosystem Nexus:
Linking climate adaptation with ecosystem restoration.
Dryland Agriculture Innovation:
Testing climate-smart farming and intercropping systems.
HydroBioSpace Model:
A new framework for integrating water, food, energy, and carbon systems.

Partnerships

Collaborative Networks

We collaborate with universities, research institutes, governments, and communities across Africa and globally to co-produce knowledge that is both scientifically rigorous and locally relevant.
